# 11 — Field and Hunter Account Motions

## Why this matters

The highest-value use of Databricks Quest is not internal training. It is a repeatable, differentiated field motion that helps prospects experience Databricks by solving real problems.

## Sales motion framing

Traditional demo:

> Watch us show you what Databricks can do.

Quest GameDay:

> Bring your team. We will give you a realistic business challenge. You will solve it hands-on in Databricks. The platform will validate the outcome.

This creates:

- active participation
- champion identification
- technical proof
- buyer urgency
- measurable learning
- opportunity signals

## Hunter-account motion types

### 1. Data Intelligence Activation

Audience:

- CIO
- CDO
- Head of Data
- analytics leaders

Scenario:

Teams turn fragmented business data into governed, AI-ready intelligence.

Databricks capabilities:

- Unity Catalog
- SQL Warehouse
- AI/BI Dashboards
- Genie
- Lakeflow

Outcome:

Customer experiences the platform from ingestion to trusted AI/BI.

### 2. GenAI/RAG/Agents Challenge

Audience:

- CTO
- Head of AI
- digital/product teams
- data science leaders

Scenario:

Teams build a governed knowledge assistant or agent over enterprise data.

Databricks capabilities:

- Vector Search
- Model Serving
- AI Playground / serving endpoints
- Unity Catalog
- MLflow
- Apps

Outcome:

Customer sees how Databricks operationalizes GenAI with governed data.

### 3. Lakehouse Modernization Challenge

Audience:

- data engineering
- platform teams
- enterprise architecture

Scenario:

Teams modernize legacy batch SQL into governed Delta/Lakeflow pipelines.

Databricks capabilities:

- Delta Lake
- Lakeflow Declarative Pipelines
- Jobs/Workflows
- data quality expectations
- lineage

Outcome:

Customer sees practical migration path and productivity.

### 4. Governance and Trust Challenge

Audience:

- CISO
- data governance
- risk/compliance
- platform owners

Scenario:

Teams secure, classify, govern, and share sensitive data.

Databricks capabilities:

- Unity Catalog
- grants
- row/column controls
- lineage
- clean rooms / sharing if available

Outcome:

Customer sees governance as an accelerator, not a blocker.

### 5. Cost and Performance Challenge

Audience:

- platform owners
- FinOps
- engineering leadership

Scenario:

Teams diagnose expensive workloads and optimize compute/query performance.

Databricks capabilities:

- system tables
- SQL profiling
- serverless warehouses
- liquid clustering
- Lakehouse Monitoring if applicable

Outcome:

Customer sees practical cost control and observability.

## Account planning workflow

1. Identify target account use case.
2. Select quest pack.
3. Customize narrative and datasets.
4. Identify target personas.
5. Run event with mixed technical/business teams.
6. Capture completion and blockers.
7. Produce post-event account insight report.
8. Convert insights into next-step workshop/POC.

## Post-event sales signals

Capture:

- which personas participated
- which teams moved fastest
- which capabilities generated excitement
- which tasks failed most often
- which blockers map to customer pain
- which users became informal champions
- what follow-up use case was requested

Every hunter motion quest pack should include:

- executive one-pager
- facilitator guide
- technical setup guide
- runbook
- quest manifest
- sample data
- validation dry-run checklist
- debrief deck outline
- post-event report template

## What makes this credible

The app should not simulate Databricks. It should validate real Databricks work inside a workspace.

Credibility comes from:

- real workspace actions
- real table/object validation
- real system telemetry
- real leaderboards
- real evidence
- post-event analytics
